Introduction

Data Analysis



In the era of pandemic, social media plays an important role in daily life, becoming the main way for people to understand the world and helping people contact others and expand their circles. Nearly 30% of consumers hope to establish more intimate connections through social media. From the perspective of marketing, social media has important analytical significance, and advertising strategies need to be adjusted accordingly through its trends and data.

Analysing social media data becomes a good primary source for social research. Tweet enables us to gain insight into online public behaviours and analysing Twitter has become an important source of information for brands and agents.

Unlike other social media platform indexes, Twitter API is open and accessible, so Twitter becomes more suitable tools for developers to get access to the data, which increases the possibility of researchers using developing and evaluating informatics tools.

At the same time, Twitter data is easy to retrieve, because the main events, news stories and events on Twitter are usually used with hashtags. Searching hashtags directly can easily attain the relevant data.

Collection

Collecting the data

It is easy to collect data and run multiple archives by setting up tags only once, which simplifies the procedures of connection with Twitter; and tags can directly create a new Google form to collect and store the data with better capacity and performance.

The number of collected tweets must be between a few thousand to 2 million to make the data most suitable for analysis. Too much or too little will cause corresponding problems. Therefore, by using "FREEBLACKPINK /BlinkPrioritizeMusic/ GlobeBLACKPINK/ BlackpinkDontCareAboutBlinks" as keywords, the most relevant information can be collected most effectively, and different hashtags can be connected through OR to filter out all the data containing four hashtags. trying to use #blackpink to search, there are the topic is too hot in Twitter, with almost a million pieces of data every day, so too many tweets are not conducive to analysis as a topic. Too few tweets for a single keyword and insufficient data may cause bias in the results.

From December 27th, 2020 to December 29th, 2020, a total of 25,490 data was collected on this topic, including 5,985 original tweets and 19,505 re-tweets. Obviously, 76.52% of the data comes from retweets, which shows that people prefer to retweet on this topic.

The time series of tweets are below.
It is clear to see that there was a rapid decrease in the number of tweets after 8 am, December 28th. Starting from almost 2500 people tweeting around 22:00 on December 27th in 2020, this figure rose signifcantly to a peak of about over 3500.

Cleaning

Cleaning the data

In order to confirm the popularity and accuracy of the data, we must first exclude robots and official accounts. However, due to the particularity of this topic, fans were creators who established the hashtags to complain against YG, so there is no official account in this topic, and only robots need to be excluded. The first step is using the tool called Botometer to preliminary screen the robot, and then searching for the user on Twitter. Observing the total number of tweets, content and frequency of tweets is an effective way to find the robot.

Among the top 10 users on this topic, only two are bots, namely KPhiannapa and Anglica53495472. KPhiannapa has joined Twitter for a short time, who joined April 2020, but a total of 6,436 Tweets have been published and only has 6 following and 2 followers, indicating this user is a robot. Another user, named Anglica53495472, who joined in November in 2020, has published for 4208 tweets. Although she has 15 followers, there is not enough evidence to show that this account is not a robot.
